FAQs


1. What is VootHolidays?

It is a travel support and assistant portal, where you can check the fares and compare the deals to grab the right one for your trip. Travel booking is not so difficult for flyers when they get assistance from the right platform.

2. What is the logic of choosing the cheapest day to book a flight?

It is the proper method to consider the right deal and destination. Your booking and travel flexibility decide the percentage of discount that you can grab from any website. However, the logic of the cheapest day to book a flight works for both domestic and international travel.

3. Which is the cheapest day to find flight deals?

No doubt it is a weekday because weekends are busiest and expensive as well for booking and travel. You can see, people who choose weekends to fly or book airline tickets always pay an extra amount of money for airline reservations, rather than those people who are flexible with their arrival and departure dates.

4. How to book cheap flight tickets?

You can book the cheap flight tickets while being flexible, booking tickets on weekdays, flying during the off-season time, and booking the tickets in advance. These are some significant ways that you can choose to buy cheap tickets for your destination.

5. Are airlines or travel booking websites like VootHolidays tracking my searches to raise prices?

Is the "clear your cookies" trick right?
Well, the answer is no, because sometimes people think that this trick is right. After all, this is one of the biggest myths in travel. You can also check by yourself by clearing your cookies. Airlines use an incredibly complex pricing system based on supply and demand, and historical data does not affect their pricing at all. The price changes from time to time because a fare bucket sells out, or the airline algorithm adjusts the price for everyone, not just for you. The real reason behind the price change is that it is dynamic and changes constantly. At the same time, cookies are used for ads.

6. Is there really a "magic day" to book flights, like Tuesday at 1 PM?

Another big myth that you need to avoid is the magic day to book flights. It is true that many airlines announce their fare sales every week to sell out all the tickets, but often launch early in the week, and modern dynamic pricing still works. The modern rule is that the cheapest days to fly are far more critical than the cheapest day to book. On the other hand, flying on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Saturday is almost always more affordable than flying on Friday and Sunday. Your strategy should focus on flexibility rather than on the cheapest days.
